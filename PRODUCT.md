# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Individuals tracking habits, tasks and goals for themselves, primarily on their phone (one-hand use, quick glances, checking a habit in under 3 seconds). They open the app several times a day in short bursts: morning routine, commute, before bed. A future native store app will reuse this design language. The owner also uses it daily and showcases it as a portfolio piece.

## Product Purpose

Streek makes daily consistency feel rewarding. Users check habits, complete tasks and reach goals; the app turns that into visible momentum: streaks, XP, levels, a flame that "heats up". Success = the user comes back tomorrow. v1 ships end of July 2026; shipping beats perfection.

## Brand Personality

Warm, energetic, playful. The flame is the identity: warmth in every neutral, orange as the hero color, juicy feedback on key actions (check = pop + light confetti). Duolingo/Headspace energy but with taste: playful, never childish. Copy is short, encouraging, first-person friendly.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards: identical white cards with icon + heading + text, cold grays, corporate emptiness.
- Clinical health apps: sterile white/teal, medical tone, impersonal calm.
- Loud free-to-play gamification: golden badges, neon, confetti everywhere, visual overload. Feedback is juicy at the moment of action, quiet the rest of the time.

## Design Principles

1. **The flame is the story.** Streak momentum is the emotional core; visual heat (color intensity, gradients) should map to real user consistency, never pure decoration.
2. **Thumb-first.** Key actions live in the bottom half of the screen, touch targets ≥ 44px, one-hand flows.
3. **Juicy where it counts, calm everywhere else.** Micro-delight on the check action; the rest of the UI stays legible and warm-quiet.
4. **Warm, never cold.** No pure black/white, no cold grays; every neutral is tinted toward the flame hue.
5. **Ship it.** Prefer the simple, polished version over the ambitious, unfinished one.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` (confetti and pop animations already disabled).
- Touch targets ≥ 44px.
- Both light (default) and dark themes fully supported.
- Color is never the only signal for state (icons + text accompany it).
